Muhammad Yunus

The man who gave the poor a chance

Here's a timeline of Muhammad Yunus' life:

  • 1940 - Born in Bathua, Bangladesh
  • 1957 - Enrolled in the Department of Economics at Dhaka University, completing his BA in 1960 and MA in 1961
  • 1961 - Joined the Bureau of Economics at Dhaka University as a research assistant to economists Nurul Islam and Rehman Sobhan
  • 1965 - Received a Fulbright scholarship to study in the United States
  • 1971 - Obtained his PhD in economics from the Vanderbilt University Graduate Program in Economic Development
  • 1972 - Returned to Bangladesh and was appointed to the government's Planning Commission
  • 1976 - During visits to the poorest households in the village of Jobra near Chittagong University, Yunus discovered that very small loans could make a disproportionate difference to a poor person. The idea of microcredit was born
  • 1982 - The 28,000 member pilot project Yunus set up to lend to the poor becomes a full-fledged bank, and is renamed Grameen Bank. The success of the microfinance model led to inspiring 100 developing countries to do the same
  • 1996 - served as an advisor to the caretaker government led by former Chief Justice Muhammad Habibur Rahman, overseeing the Ministry of Primary and Mass Education, the Ministry of Science and Technology, and the Ministry of Environment and Forests
  • 2006 - Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize, along with Grameen Bank, for their efforts to create economic and social development
  • 2007 - Became an Elder alongside Nelson Mandela and Desmond Tutu as a founding member, "to contribute their wisdom, independent leadership and integrity to tackle some of the world's toughest problems."
  • 2007, 2009 - Received the Presidential Medal of Freedom and Congressional Gold Medal, respectively. This makes Yunus 1 of 7 people in history to receive those awards, along with the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 2024 - Appointed as the transitional leader of the interim government of Bangladesh as Chief Adviser

"Muhammad Yunus has shown himself to be a leader who has managed to translate visions into practical action for the benefit of millions of people, not only in Bangladesh, but also in many other countries. Loans to poor people without any financial security had appeared to be an impossible idea. From modest beginnings three decades ago, Yunus has, first and foremost through Grameen Bank, developed micro-credit into an ever more important instrument in the struggle against poverty."

-- Norwegian Nobel Committee
